The principles of necessity and proportionality when the DWP requests bank statements.

The DWP must have a legal basis before they can look at anyone’s financial information, and that basis must match a specific purpose, such as checking a particular part of a benefit claim. Even then, they can only ask for information that is necessary to answer that specific question. They are not allowed to collect extra details “just in case.” The rule of proportionality means they must choose the least intrusive option and avoid asking for more personal data than the situation genuinely requires. Finally, legal scope sets the limits of what the law actually allows them to request; they cannot go beyond those powers or use broad requests to gather unrelated information. In everyday terms, the DWP must stay within the law, explain why they need something, and only ask for the smallest amount of information needed for that exact purpose.

You have every right to make this challenge, It has no impact on outcomes if you submit Bank Statements before the deadline.

Scope, Legal Basis And Necessity Request I am writing to request clarification regarding the recent demand for copies of my bank statements. The initial framing — “Make sure you’re getting the right benefits” — appears inconsistent with the intrusive nature of the request, which in practice amounts to a compliance exercise rather than supportive guidance. While financial information can be relevant to verifying entitlement, a clear explanation is needed of the specific purpose for requesting bank statements, the exact period required, and how the information will be used in the assessment process. Clarification is also required on how personal and financial data will be handled in line with the Data Protection Act 2018 and the UK GDPR. A written explanation should set out the legal and regulatory basis for the request, define the scope of the DWP’s powers, and demonstrate the necessity of obtaining this level of financial detail by reference to the facts, evidence, and policy considerations relevant to the case. This is essential for transparency, accountability.
