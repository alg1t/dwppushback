About

This site is designed to help claimants understand their rights when the DWP asks for personal and financial information, especially bank statements. It explains key ideas like legal basis, necessity, and proportionality in plain language, and offers template wording you can adapt for your own letters. Nothing here is formal legal advice, but it is intended to give you clearer language, more confidence, and a starting point for asking fair questions about how your data is used.

Template paragraph you can reuse

I understand that you may need some financial information to assess my claim, but I expect any request to be limited to what is strictly necessary and supported by a clear legal basis. Please explain which law or regulation you are relying on, what specific purpose my information will be used for, and why the scope of the request is proportionate to that purpose. I am willing to cooperate within those limits, but I do not consent to any wider use, retention, or sharing of my data beyond what is required for the lawful assessment of my claim.

Support This Project

My motivation for putting this site together to offer accessible information for people who may feel unsure or overwhelmed when dealing with DWP requests. Many people from more marginalised groups experience the system as difficult or intimidating, so the aim here is simply to provide supportive guidance that may help people feel more confident and better equipped to respond.
