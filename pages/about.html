About

This site is designed to help claimants understand their rights when the DWP asks for personal and financial information such as bank statements. It explains key ideas like legal basis, necessity, and proportionality in plain language, and offers template wording you can adapt for your own letters. Nothing here is formal legal advice, but it is intended to give you clearer language, more confidence, and a starting point for asking fair questions about how your data is used.

Motivation

In an era marked by increasing DWP overreach — including the rise of algorithmic sanctions imposed without adequate due process, intrusive demands for bank statements, routine privacy violations, and a pattern of claimant harassment — many individuals find themselves ill‑equipped to navigate systems that have become both opaque and adversarial. These developments unfold against a wider societal backdrop in which marginalised groups are increasingly subjected to denigration, all within the continuing legacy of austerity that has eroded public trust and institutional accountability.

The motivation for this platform arises from the rapid expansion of modern technological systems, particularly AI‑driven tools embedded within government decision‑making processes. These systems now exert significant influence over people’s lives, often in ways that are impersonal, unchallengeable, and unjust. As both public and private bodies adopt automated mechanisms to assess, monitor, and control claimants, individuals are frequently left disempowered by processes they cannot question, scrutinise, or even understand.

This platform seeks to address that imbalance. By developing accessible resources, guides, and templates, it aims to equip claimants with the knowledge and tools necessary to resist automated injustices, challenge unfair decisions, and hold institutions to account. The broader purpose is to help restore transparency, accountability, and human dignity in administrative environments where technology has too often been used to undermine them, and to contribute to a culture of informed, lawful resistance to the increasingly authoritarian deployment of AI within public and private governance.>
