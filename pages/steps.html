Action Implemntation Prior To Statement Submission

Even if a claimant provides bank statements, it remains firmly in their best interests to complete these preliminary steps. Taking them now creates a strong evidential foundation should the situation escalate, whether through further DWP action, ICO involvement, or any form of legal challenge. This kind of early future‑proofing ensures the claimant is protected, organised, and able to demonstrate that they acted reasonably and proportionately throughout.

Create An Audit Trail

Creating an audit trial is especially important because it gives you proof of what you told the DWP and when, making it easier to challenge errors or delays. If you request a mandatory reconsideration or make a complaint, your logs and screenshots show you cooperated fully and sent all requested evidence, which strengthens your case and counters any claims against you.

• Keep a written log of every contact with the DWP, noting the date, time, how you contacted them (phone, online, letter), who you spoke to, and what was said or agreed.
• Save copies of all letters, emails, and forms you send or receive, and take screenshots of online messages or uploads so you can prove exactly what you sent and when.
• Store these records in one place (for example, a dedicated folder or notebook) so you can quickly refer to them if there is a dispute, a complaint, or a request for evidence later on.

Account Declaration And Captital Verification

When the DWP requests bank statements or other financial information, it’s important to respond promptly and cooperatively. Supplying information such as account declarations and balances (They will have them anyway) prior to any actual bank statement submission. This helps avoid delays and shows that you are fully engaging with the process. Even if the request feels intrusive or confusing, keeping communication polite and timely ensures the DWP cannot portray you as being uncooperative or dismissive. Taking this approach protects your position and helps your claim move forward as smoothly as possible.

Declaration Of Medical Conditions

Always declare any medical conditions that may affect your ability to obtain and submit bank statements within the DWP's requested timeframe. This proactive step allows them to consider reasonable adjustments or extensions under equality laws, preventing claims of non-cooperation while protecting your entitlement and avoiding payment suspensions.

Obtaining Justification For The Requests

After completing the earlier steps, the next thing to do is ask the DWP to explain why they want your bank statements and what legal reason they believe supports their request. You are fully entitled to ask for this information, and the DWP has a duty to be open and clear about their decisions. You still have the right to know exactly why the information is needed and how it relates to your claim. The difficulty at this stage is the conveniently short deadline the DWP often gives for a response, which can discourage claimants from seeking proper justification. For that reason, it’s best to take these steps as soon as possible.
